1) Write about your experience playing, what happened during play?

The game was definitely an experience playing! I chose Indiana Jones because I was a big fan of the movies and modern games like Uncharted 4. The first thing I immediately noticed is how much I took for granted how smooth the controls for modern games are (even the ones more notorious for being janky to play.)

The extra amount of pixels offered in modern screens allow for much more precise movements and its really noticeable ingame with Indiana Jones where it feels like the character being controlled moves a bazillion units of distance or whatever with a slight nudge it seems. Also I guess hitboxes are much less refined and forgiving than modern games which I think are more generous for the sake of being more fun. You said in class that many older games were intentionally much harder than games made today.

2) How is it different playing on an emulator vs playing the game on the original hardware.

For this game in particular I did not find it challenging to play on the emulator at all. A huge part of that though was I kind of lucked out choosing the game I chose and I came to realize that after playing my game and browsing some other ones I could have potentially chose instead. My game only had a four movement buttons and an attack button while other games literally had like a bazillion different buttons for a bazillion different actions. The Star Trek game with the vector art had like almost 12 or 15 different buttons to operate a spaceship!

Crazy that all fit in a cabinet game! As for the experience, well of course playing on a laptop will never be the “authentic” experience of playing inside an arcade with all the flashing lights and excitement!
